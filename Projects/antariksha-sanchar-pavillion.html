Antariksha Sanchar is India’s first major cultural video game. It is a point and click adventure inspired by the dream theorems of prodigious mathematician Srinivasa Ramanujan, and originating from an opera by the classical dancer Jayalakshmi Eshwar.

The game follows a simple yet remarkable family living in the early 1900s in a temple town inspired by the city of Madurai, as they construct a new philosophy blending ancient culture, spirituality, natural creation, science and synaesthesia. The project is an engaging, educative and surprising peek into Indian culture interpreted by an emerging generation of Indian media artists.

The game is currently in it’s second phase of development and has spawned a dance opera, graphic novel and Augmented Reality experiences

Although the project originally started off in 2011 as an audio-visual dance performance in collaboration with Jayalakshmi Eshawar, it has since continued development with various Quicksand team members contributing in different ways.

Process

I have been involved in the research and documentation for the game and dance performance, design of the graphic novel, the Augmented Reality experiences and exhibitions of the game.
